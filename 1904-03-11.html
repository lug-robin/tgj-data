The Holy One, Blessed is He, does not exact retribution from a nation until its time is up. (Tractate Sotah, Chapter 9)

The ruler of over 130 million people in Europe and Asia is weeping in his winter palace, as telegrams report from every corner, because he is helpless, like a child. He is sitting, wringing his hands and sobs bitter tears like the Jewish widows and orphans of the Kishinev pogroms.

Yes, the tsar is weeping.

It has not been a year yet, since the tragedy on the eighth day of Passover. There are still five weeks to go until the first anniversary. The luxury.

(Second article)

In my first article about luxury in “The Genuine Jew” I described briefly in which ways luxury is a blight of Galician Jewry. Whether the article has already changed something, if it opened someone’s eyes is to be debated. The public is entrenched in bitter and heavy suffering that it does not know where it is hurting. And as there are several who feel and know how harmful it is, they need to shut up. They are afraid to say something, they know that they want to scratch their eyes out when seeing the femininity of the Jewish daughters. What are they supposed to do? They watch everything in chopped motions and stay silent.

To remedy this, they have to look for a way to enlighten those who are addicted to fashion how bad it is for them. Only saying or writing it once will not convince them. One has to say it a lot, to scream a lot and scream loudly until they understand it. Then it will be achieved to enlighten the populace. Perhaps God will have mercy!

Lately we are being suppressed from every side, meeting our needs has become difficult or it is completely taken away even. The neighbours are looking for ways to take away the Jewish livelihoods with “village collections”, with “cooperative societies”, and so on. The number of antisemites grow continuously to every side, their wants do as well. They do not want to lose against others. Furthermore, we Jews are searching for new taxes to pay as well. Under no circumstances can we let it happen that a government wants a tax of several guilds from every Jewish wedding. With what force will this be implemented? It will be recorded, protested, called upon. Every Jewish as well as Jewish-adjacent newspaper will write lengthy articles. Unbelievable! Has this not been heard before! We are also taxing ourselves high. Every smaller wedding receives 20 - 30 telegrams, the opulent weddings receive 100 - 200 and more. They make a whole fuss out of it, but what for? Does it make any sense? It is very costly. Jews do not know where to put their money!

The high valued virtue of “hospitality” has also disappeared from a lot of Jewish houses because of luxury. In earlier times as Jews had enough riches with 2 - 3 rooms, the furniture was simple, no rugs on the floor. A person entered the house, greeted the guest warmly; he did not ruin the furniture, did not soil the rugs. On Sabbath people fought over having a poor person over for Sabbath, everyone wanted to provide their matzah and hospitality. These days, our Jews became very

[2]

different and with it the good Jewish customs. Nowadays after a wedding, they truly have many rags with thousand coins dowry. First, they rent a lovely apartment, 2 - 3 rooms with nice furniture, carpets and rugs as well as several other things. At that point they have already spent a huge part of the dowry and have already stepped towards poverty. As soon as someone enters the door, he will be looked upon as a detrimental thing, he could ruin the chairs, soil the carpets. Later, when a daughter has grown up, raised by today’s Zeitgeist, she will not like her own home. She would like to store everything anywhere. How can a Jew let someone in their home and who invites a poor person still?

In previous times, when one met a poor man, one befriended him. He would have been treated friendly and brotherly, he was given the best meals. The poor man was satisfied and could forget his poverty a bit. He started to like the rich men, the landlords that once he got him he told how the rich man treated him, how good these people are. This made a good impression on his children and they have become more humane as they realised that there are good people left in the world. These days, the poor man comes home and recounts how badly he has been treated. He is planting misanthropy, spite, and despair in the young hearts of his children. Because of this the children will later in life, when they are older, be driven into useless and harmful tendencies which will neither provide help or honour to them or their brothers.

Letters from London.

It happened as we have predicted before: We turn up and right to listen to the speech of the throne for the parliament’s inauguration. The main venture of the government for this season is to put through a bill which should forbid workers to enter and unless the government breaks up in the middle, this law is virtually certain.

In the meantime, the law’s details are not public yet. However, the newspapers which are close to the government and constantly know what they are talking about published the character of these new laws. It turns out that the government wants to make them very strict. As the papers claim, that before immigrants are allowed to enter England they will have to provide:

  • A valid passport issued by the countries they resided in before.
  • He must know how to read and write in the language of origin.
  • He must have a certain amount of money and will have to explain how he plans on earning money.
  • The immigrant has to be completely healthy.

    • If the immigrant cannot satisfy the inspectors in these four points he will be sent back.

    But it does not end here. If the immigrant is lucky enough to be admitted into the country, they are allowed to stay 3 - 5 days under police surveillance. If it turns out that an immigrant lives on welfare - except in times of sickness - or breaks a law, he will immediately be sent back to the country of origin.

    Those areas suffering or had suffered from an abundance of foreigners, is allowed to be classified as a forbidden areas, where foreign Jews are not allowed to live.

    In order to protect it all, foreigners living in England have to report themselves to the police. No foreigner is allowed to move places without telling the police in order for the latter to know whether the foreigner lives legally or illegally.

    This is supposed to be the government’s plan; a “Jewish quarter” should be erected like the ones in Russia, but a little bit worse. First this should prevent new migrants from moving there and meeting the ones already residing there. The new Jew should not be able to have job prospects and should not be able to find other Jews. And when they find stone instead of bread how would they be able to find work or an occupation in good Jewish neighbourhoods since the assaults on Jews everywhere in the world and in Ireland in the last few weeks.

    The rich Jews understand that the new legislation only targets Jews. They know it’s the result of a weak government which bends to the wishes of the British brothers to gain votes for the upcoming election. The Jews in high ranks do everything possible to prevent the law from becoming so strict. If the law has to be passed it should at least be as mild as possible. One can only hope that until the law is passed, it will be altered a lot. In any case, it will be much stricter than the American immigration laws. The laws in America want to target only truly bad immigrants although good ones are also often affected. Nobody wants to exclude good workers. It is still mainly affecting the good ones to prevent fierce competition against the domestic workers.

    English citizens in some areas of London took the minister’s advice to defend themselves from foreigners with their fists. In a particular London area, a gang has been formed to beat up every Jew they meet on the street; Jewish workers have been pelted with stones on the way from and to work. Police are constantly not present to arrive at the scene once it is too late and the gang has dispersed already.

    The Austrian Imperial Assembly.

    The Austrian parliament opened its doors again on Tuesday. Minister president Dr. Koerber held a great political speech in the first meeting. He reported about the war between Russia and Japan and noted that Austria-Hungary commiserates the war and will remain

    [3]

    strictly neutral - impartial to both parties. After that he discussed the Balkan’s state of affairs and said that any rumours about mobilisation of the Austrian army is false. He hopes to bring order to Turkey without war.

    Then he talked about the Austrian state of affairs. He announced that the government is planning to build an Italian university in the Tyrolean city of Rovereto and called upon the parliament to finally get to work as the country is suffering greatly from the parliament’s inactivity to pass important legislation. Over 60 laws which have to be passed under § 14 lay in parliament gathering dust. Nobody can conclude new trade contracts, the new custom tariff has not been agreed upon, the Compromise is hanging on thin threads. Subsequently the minister president urged the parliament to first conclude the new recruitment law. He does not expect for war to break out on the Balkan, but Austria has to be prepared for any eventualities.

    The speech did not have a great impact. The Young Czech Party is not willing to stop their obstruction. They requested to open the session over the minister president's speech to spend some days with it. They also initiated some emergency motions which have to be managed first according to the rules of order. Therefore, the hope to debate the recruitment act is diminished.

    During the second session, the minister president answered to the demands coming from the demonstrations between Germans and Czechs in Prague last Sunday. The Czechs do not permit German students to stroll through Prague with their colourful hats and ribbons. Prague is a Slavic city and must not be “colonised” by Germans. The Germans do not want to relent and that is why Czechs and Germans clashed intensely last Sunday. The police and military had to intervene, several people were arrested, Prague looked like a revolution. The demonstrations happened again in the middle of the week. The current situation is to be taken very seriously. The Czechs were not satisfied with the minister president’s answer and caused a scandal in parliament. They yelled, rattled, and whistled - like in bygone days.

    Little hope remains that the parliament can truly return to doing serious work. The government either has to resign and a new one has to install peace and order; or the Imperial Assembly will be dissolved and new elections will be arranged. Maybe the new parliament will be better than the old one.

    Cancer

    Cancer is one of the most dangerous and most serious illnesses. Healing cancer is impossible. For now the only way to save a person from death is surgery, but surgery only helps in the first stage of cancer when it hasn’t spread through the body. Or if the cancer is located in a limb which can be cut off completely. Unfortunately, cancer is almost never found in the early stages; when it is found it has usually progressed far. And the risk is really high.

    Cancer leads to death either by destroying an important part of the body or by debilitating the body or by releasing vigorous poison into the flesh. The tumour is easy to cut out with a knife, but it will soon spread out in new tumours. Professor Billroth is the first who managed to cut out cancer of the innermost parts of the body. But the cancer is hardly ever cut out completely, the majority remains in the body. Either it is not found or the tumour takes over too much space in the body and the surgeon has to put their knife down. Cancer grows well in the human body and spreads more and more until it is lethal.

    Cancer can grow in any part of the human body. Men and women get cancer, but women suffer in greater percentages. However, mainly older people develop cancer.

    Where the illness comes from is not known yet. Looking at the tumour through a microscope, one can see that it is made up of small cells which grow in the human flesh. The cells grow and multiply intensely, they spread with the ordinary cells of which the human flesh consists of and through which blood and other fluids are transported through the body. The cancer grows and grows until only cancer remains in the body. Then it starts to swell and decay.

    Why and how it is growing those cells in the body - that question still remains unanswered. Lately, a theory goes around that cancer stems from particularly small beings (microbes) which can neither be spotted with the blind eye nor with current enlargers. They transmitted the cancer onto other creatures to prove that cancer, like any contagious disease, only stems from microbes. This hypothesis proves that the microbes causing tumors are particular ones and if those microbes are cultivated the illness can be transferred to others. This hypothesis has been put out into the world several times already, but stricter research proved that it is too early to support this claim. In any case, this theory that special microbes cause cancer is becoming more certain. At the last medical congress in Wiesbaden, professor Leyden said to have found the cancer microbe. Prof. Leyden reckoned that cancer growth can only be explained by living organisms. The spread of cancer, he said, is the same as every other disease of which we know. It has to stem from microbes. Cancer causes the same change in the blood and the same bodily depletion. Prof. Leyden also provides proof how the cancer was spread from one living creature to another. It is very much possible that humans can also get infected but that is yet to be proven. He merely said that he had met with a young doctor who once drank the juice of a tumour. After two years the doctor got sick - stomach cancer. Prof. Leyden found special microbes in cancer patients which he thinks to be the cause of cancer. They are found en masse under the skin and multiply heavily and grow into a big blister. This blister bursts and new blisters with new microbes form.

    If Leyden’s theory can be proven, it will have a great impact. Not only is the cause known, but the possibility of healing grows.

    Researching cancer is becoming more and more important, because more and more get sick from cancer. In 1892, two out of every 100 dying in Germany died of cancer. In 1898, it already increased to three. Calculated differently, out of 100,000 living people 60 died of cancer in 1892. In 1898 this number rose to 71. In a period of seven years cancer grew to 18 %.

    The same proliferation of cancer diseases were also found in other countries. In England, out of every million living people, 384 people died of cancer from 1861 to 1870. In the next ten years 468, in the next 589 and in the last decade 829. In Holland, 49 people out of every 100,000 died of cancer in 1874, in 1897 114 died, and in 1898 the number grew to 132. In New York 2,473 people died of cancer in 1888 and 4117 in 1893.

    These numbers show that the disease is multiplying more and more and starting to affect younger people more as well.

    Because of the danger and increasing number of cancer patients, scholars are interested more in cancer. Several capable doctors and professors are researching the disease. Even specialised science committees have been founded to collect material to research the disease. It is important to know whether the sickness can be spread from human to human, if it can be inherited from parents to children and so on. The spread of the disease has become important for everyone and more and more private people are donating huge sums to scientific studies of cancer.

    If more knowledge about the disease is collected, where it comes from and how it grows, there could also be a way to fight against it.

    Last News.

    A great win for Japan.

    Berlin. The local paper “Berliner Lokal-Anzeiger" received a telegram in the last hour. In Korea, 80 kilometres away from the city Pyongyang where the Japanese main battalions were stationed, the Russians and Japanese erupted into a great battle. Japan won gloriously.

    Russia wants to withdraw.

    London. The Russian ambassador in London, Count Benckendorff went to St Petersburg at the start of the war already where he stayed for a few days before he went back to London. Since his return, he went to King Edward and, as it is rumoured in political circles, wants him to take care of the peace mediation between Russia and Japan. Russia wants to retreat from Manchuria and hand Port Arthur over to Japan. That is why it should conclude peace.

    Victory of the government in Hungary.

    Budapest. The Hungarian government achieved a massive goal in the parliament. As the opposition noticed that the government was serious with changing its rules of order and was also ready to enforce this change with violence in order to render the obstruction impossible, the opposition conceded. The minister president Tisza retracted his motion to change the rules, but the opposition obliged to sign off the recruitment act and other important laws. (Read the article “Hungary’s situation” in the Tuesday feature.)
