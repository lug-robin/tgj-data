Our religious education

“One could almost laugh about it, if it wasn’t so sad”... We can justifiably use this general idiom in regards to the religious education at our public schools when accusing our advanced Jewish people that our religious education is substantially neglected. Our children grow up without a Jewish consciousness concerning religion and faith, they do not know anything about the Jewry, about Jewish celebrations, or about Jewish music. The educated Jewry then points to the religious education in the schools, that a religious educator is employed in every school who is paid by the Jewish community, that a representative of the Jewish community sits in the local education authority, that according to the law the religious education is crucial in education. A student might be perfectly educated, but if he doesn’t know about religion he will not pass the exam - because of all of that they know that the religious education is esteemed and highly renowned and everything is to be done in order to educate the children religiously.

All of those responses are correct. We have religious educators in every school - but nobody asks what kind of educators they are? We have representatives in the local education authorities, but nobody asks how concerned they are with our religious inquiries? - The Jewish communities pay the teacher, but nobody asks how they care about the religious education? - The religious education is a significant subject for every Jewish pupil, but nobody asks if it really happened that a Jewish student failed an exam because he didn’t know anything about religion?

The answer is: religious education in schools is a serious subject, but is not taught seriously. We don’t want to use a harsher expression, otherwise we would have to say: it is treated like a joke.

The religious educator is for the most part not really pious. The children realise that he thinks little of religion and what he teaches. Therefore, the religious education doesn’t leave a big impression, they don’t take it seriously and take religion solely as a story, which they don’t have to know anything about, like other historical chronicles. There isn’t really an alternative; for the most part they are in school ignoring the Sabbath to learn writing, calculating and similar - how should they think about the Sabbath as honourable? And if the religious educator is a serious man, as those educators really are, then he has a serious challenge with children of non-pious parents. For example, if he learns in school that eating Chametz is not allowed during Passover - and that child goes home and eats Chametz , then goes back to school and explains that the teacher lied about Chametz not being forbidden to eat .The same happens with the Sabbath observance and other big customs of the Jewish religion.

But if a teacher who wants to take a stance against that and wants to convince the children that the religion he teaches is the true Jewry - then a fight against him rises. He gets reported to the Local Education Administration, an investigation against him gets instigated and he is being assaulted until he shuts up and is quiet.

However, a Jewish representative sits in the Local Education Administration, who should take on the teacher, because doesn’t he sit there for exactly that reason? You will of course ask, but that is indeed a bad thing. The Jewish school inspector wants to demonstrate that he is a modern man, not a fanatic and only cares about the religion teacher if pious parents complain that the lessons are bad. But he will remain quiet if someone complains that the teacher is too pious (usually a lawyer or a doctor or similar who has little knowledge of religion and is hardly pious), nobody should think only he is a “fanatic” under no circumstances…

Not that anyone now thinks we are telling fairy tales, that we are suspected of lies. We want to bring facts which have been created in a big jewish community here in Galicia.

A Jewish religious educator who came to the knowledge that Jewish students are sitting in school on Sabbath, asked the director he shall free the children from writing on Sabbath, as the law does allow that. The director sent this inquiry to the LEA, which sent it to the board of the religious community, if writing on Sabbath is indeed prohibited. This inquiry has had an answer from the religious board for a few months now. They do not have the audacity to say they should be writing; to say no on the other shames him, in case someone might say the board are fanatics!... We think that it is a bigger shame to be silent about this for so long.

A second case happened in the same city: A Jewish teacher talked about the Law of Moses, the children reported this at home, some pious Jewish people told the school inspector and he initiated an investigation and interrogated the children as witnesses. The Jewish doctor who is the representative in the LEA, who should have been most angered about the Jewish teacher, helped her and wanted to twist the children's answers with various questions in order to make several pull back their statements. He didn't achieve this, but this case shows how far the interests of the Jewish representatives in the LEA go for the Jewish religion.

Are those two tales proof enough, or should we describe more stories? - We don’t think that as necessary: every Jewish reader knows stories like this of their own experience. There are very little or even no Jewish religious educators who are not complained about and there are few schools where stories like this don’t circulate.

However, we can say that the education in school according to the required methods, doesn’t get to the children. They stay unfamiliar with religion, its people and their faith.

(The End)

The Truth



Letter from Hungary

The Hungarian Jews, their lives, their political and economical situation, are not really known to our Jewish readers. The Hungarian Jew is a different “type”, he differs from the Polish and the German ones. They live miles apart and don’t come in contact with each other. I’m not looking to describe Hungarian Jewish people in detail, their situation, their conditions and their states. I will do that in another letter. I just want to share important information which will interest every Jew.

In Hungary live - beside Magyars - several other tribes, like Germans, Romanians, Slowakians, Croatians, Ruthenians and others. Together they would be the majority in the whole populace and the Magyars do know that if all those tribes get together, Hungary would not be a Magyarianian state, nor a Slavish one. Therefore they strive to strengthen the Magyarian populace, suppress the other nationalities, refuse them their national pride and don’t let them develop their national interests. The Jewish people in Hungary are always loyal to the government, supporting it in every situation and above all, the government has always got massive support during elections in the cities, where many Jewish people live among Romanians and Slavic people. Without Jewish people, Slavic people would have been elected into the government. Where few Hungarians live, Jewish people help Hungarians to a successful election.

Furthermore, the Jewish people in Hungary can influence the government in certain ways and if they want something, they have the power to do so. Still, there have been some cases where the government issued laws, which harmed Jewish people there. The last minister president Kálmán Széll allowed Christians to open shops and banks in frontier districts where a lot of Jewish people live among Ruthenians and Slavs in order to support the populace there. That harmed the Jewish people there. As soon as they opposed it, the government had to stop. Jewish people seem to have a bit more influence in Hungary compared to other countries.

This influence also proved useful in the case of the Sunday rest. Many pious Jewish people, especially in Galicia, suffer under the law of Sunday Rest. They close their shop on Sabbath and also have to do so on Sunday. Closing their stores two days a week is difficult, especially for the little merchant. The Hungarian Jewish people managed to achieve some relief.

A few months ago, the government issued a decree for every tobacco shop to be open for business on Sabbath. The licence is to be revoked of those who keep it closed on Sabbath. This decree has been a serious “punishment”. Several tobacconists, who didn’t want their licence to be revoked, and have therefore violated Sabbath. However, many pious Jewish people insisted and have not lost their livelihood over this. The Jewish people have raised tumult: they have handed over petitions to the finance minister. Last week he gave confirmation to the chancellery of Orthodox Jews in Budapest that he meets half of their demands. That means: he issued another decree that those who ran their shops before the previous decree may close their shops on Sabbath. And those who have had their licences revoked, will get it back. Still, tobacconists who get their licence now have to vow to keep their shop open on Sabbath. In other words, pious Jewish people cannot open tobacco shops anymore…

The minister has already written to the chancellery that he cannot back down anymore and they cannot ask for more. However, the pious Jewish people of Hungary must not remain silent and have to use all means at their disposal to abolish the rest of this punishment. If the government doesn’t want to give in, they have to turn to the parliament. And if that doesn’t help as well they have to submit to our Lord, King … and they will hopefully achieve everything.

Jewish people have made Romanians and Slavs their enemies because they have always supported the government! In those places where a lot of Slavs live there is a great hostility between them and the Jewish people there which leads to fights and brawls. I do not want to judge whether the Jewish people gain advantages for themselves by being devoted to the Magyars and therefore make enemies with the other peoples. Maybe it would have been better for the Jewish people to stay neutral and not form alliances with any other nationality. I want to write about that another time. The Jewish people are already suffering because # they support the government and should therefore not make them suffer more. However, if the government forgets their duty, the Jewish people need to remind them as long as they are listened to.



The War

Between Japan and Russia

After lengthy negotiation going back and forth, the war between Japan and Russia has ultimately broken out. Both have hesitated a long time, waiting for the other one to start the one so as to not bear the responsibility of inaugurating it. Finally, Japan lost its patience and called back their ambassador from Petersburg immediately leading to war. The first few days, news from Japanese victories at sea have been received. If Japan beats Russia at sea, the war will continue on the mainland. It is not clear yet how exactly both armies will crash against each other. First of all, no one knows how fast both want to reach the capital of Korea, Seoul.
